about

The Problem

Many online and print job postings are worded in certain ways that attract and drive more men over women to apply for the position.

Our Goal

Our goal in this project is to identify and reduce such bias in certain job postings primarily in the technology sector.

Support Us

Please click on the survey button above to help us evaluate the inherent bias in certain job postings so they can be revised.

Analysis

Job: job posting that was given to participants.
Intended Bias: relative gender lean of the job posting determined by an online algorithm (as noted in our report).
Surveyed Bias I: the breakdown of those who thought that these job postings were feminine (pink), masculine (blue), or nuetral (green).
Word Changes: a list of the words originally highlighted as potentially being gender-bias and the word that was crowdsourced to replace it.
Surveyed Bias II: the breakdown of those who thought that these job postings were feminine (pink), masculine (blue), or nuetral (green) after replacing the originally highlighted words with the crowdsourced synonym for those words.
Change in Bias: if the overall nuetrality of the job posting increased (as determined by the crowd), then there will be a green down arrow. If the overall nuetrality of the job posting decreased, then there is a red up arrow.
